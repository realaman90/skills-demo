# PDF Summaries — Reputation & Employer Brand

## 1. The-employer-brand_Tim-Ambler-and-Simon-Barrow.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/The-employer-brand_Tim-Ambler-and-Simon-Barrow.pdf
**Journal:** Journal of Brand Management, 4(3), 1996

The founding paper for employer branding. Ambler & Barrow first apply brand management principles to the employment relationship — arguing that organisations should manage their identity as an employer the same way they manage product brands. Introduces the three-benefit framework: functional, economic, and psychological benefits. Establishes that psychological benefits (purpose, belonging, culture) are undervalued relative to economic benefits (pay). The conceptual foundation for the EVP.

---

## 2. Brown&Dacin.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Brown&Dacin.pdf
**Journal:** Journal of Marketing, 61(1), 1997

A seminal paper on how corporate associations affect consumer product evaluations. Introduces the CA (Corporate Ability) and CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) distinction. Shows empirically that both types of associations influence purchase intent, but through different mechanisms. CA drives quality and credibility; CSR drives warmth and advocacy. A foundational paper for understanding how corporate reputation connects to consumer behaviour and product brand performance.

---

## 3. RepTrak Pulse Measure Ponzi.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/RepTrak Pulse Measure Ponzi.pdf
**Journal:** Corporate Reputation Review, 14(1), 2011

The psychometric validation study for the RepTrak Pulse measure. Ponzi, Fombrun & Gardberg establish the four-item emotional reputation measure and validate it as a reliable, cross-nationally consistent short-form tool. Also describes how the Pulse relates to the seven rational drivers (Products & Services, Innovation, Workplace, Governance, Citizenship, Leadership, Performance). The methodological backbone of the RepTrak global rankings.

## 4. Reputation Council Ponzi Pages.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Reputation Council Ponzi Pages.pdf

Supporting RepTrak research from Ponzi's work with the Reputation Council. Extends the RepTrak model with practical guidance on using reputation data for strategic decision-making. Addresses how reputation scores connect to stakeholder behaviour outcomes (buy, recommend, work for, invest in).

## 5. Porter and Kramer (2011) Creating_Shared_Value.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Porter and Kramer (2011) Creating_Shared_Value.pdf
**Journal:** Harvard Business Review, 89(1/2), 2011

Porter and Kramer's influential reframe of corporate social responsibility. Argues businesses must reconnect their success with social progress by creating shared value — not just managing CSR. Three mechanisms: reconceiving products and markets, redefining value chain productivity, enabling local cluster development. Distinguishes CSV from philanthropy and CSR. Highly influential in repositioning sustainability and social impact as strategic business drivers rather than cost or PR activities.

## 6. The Future of Purpose-Driven Branding - David Aaker.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/The Future of Purpose-Driven Branding - David Aaker.pdf

Aaker's argument for brand purpose as a strategic differentiator. Covers the conditions for authentic purpose (rooted in brand DNA, linked to the product, credible internally), the risk of purpose washing, and how purpose connects to brand equity and talent strategy. Bridges CSV strategy with brand management practice.

## 7. Aaker_Joachimsthaler.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Aaker_Joachimsthaler.pdf

Aaker & Joachimsthaler on brand leadership — how corporate brands can lead and organise brand portfolios. Addresses the role of the corporate brand in architecture decisions (branded house, house of brands, endorsed), and how secondary associations from corporate brands transfer to product brands. Relevant for organisations managing their corporate reputation as a strategic brand asset.

---

## 8. Schein.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Schein.pdf

Edgar Schein's foundational work on organisational culture — the three-level model (artifacts, espoused values, underlying assumptions). Establishes that culture is not what organisations say they believe, but what they actually do — and that underlying assumptions are the most powerful and resistant-to-change layer. Critical for understanding the culture-reputation link: reputation follows actual culture, not stated culture.

---

## 9. Paul_Argenti.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Paul_Argenti.pdf

Argenti on corporate communication as reputation management. Covers stakeholder mapping, aligning internal identity with external image, the sequence from corporate identity to image to reputation, and how communications strategy supports reputation building. Practical framework for corporate communications professionals.

## 10. Aperia_Brönn_Schultz.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Aperia_Brönn_Schultz.pdf

Aperia, Brönn & Schultz on corporate brand and reputation management — integrating the corporate brand as a strategic asset that bridges internal culture and external stakeholder perception. Addresses how corporate brands can be managed for reputation in multi-stakeholder environments.

## 11. Understanding Reputation Risk.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Understanding Reputation Risk.pdf

A framework for understanding the sources, drivers, and mitigation of corporate reputation risk. Covers internal and external sources of reputation risk, the trust buffer effect, and crisis response principles. Useful for risk management and reputation protection planning.

## 12. Employer branding brand equity literature review.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Employer branding brand equity literature review.pdf

A comprehensive review of the employer branding and brand equity literature, synthesising research on the financial and operational outcomes of strong employer brands. Covers links between employer brand equity, recruitment efficiency, employee engagement, retention, and customer satisfaction.

## 13. Secondary_Associations.pdf
**File:** docs/newskills/09_Reputation_Employer_Brand/Secondary_Associations.pdf

Keller's framework for secondary brand associations — how brands can leverage associations from linked entities (companies, countries, events, endorsers, other brands). Directly relevant to corporate reputation: corporate associations transfer to product brands as secondary associations, and vice versa.
