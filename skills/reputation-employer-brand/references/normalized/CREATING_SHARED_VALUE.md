# Creating Shared Value (CSV)

## The Core Argument

Porter and Kramer argue that the competitiveness of a company and the health of the communities around it are mutually dependent. Creating shared value (CSV) is the idea that companies can generate economic value in a way that also produces value for society by addressing its needs and challenges.

**CSV vs. CSR:**
| Concept | Orientation | Motivation | Integration |
|---|---|---|---|
| CSR | Responding to external pressure | Reputation management, compliance | Peripheral to business strategy |
| CSV | Creating business value through social impact | Competitive advantage | Central to business strategy |

**Key distinction**: CSR is philanthropy at the periphery; CSV is strategy at the core.

---

## Three Ways to Create Shared Value

### 1. Reconceiving Products and Markets
- Design products and services that address social needs
- Identify underserved markets where social needs create new demand
- Example: healthier food products meeting nutrition needs + creating growth markets
- Example: financial products for unbanked populations

**For brand strategy**: This is the most direct link between CSV and brand — the product itself becomes the expression of shared value.

### 2. Redefining Productivity in the Value Chain
- Address social and environmental issues that affect productivity
- Energy use, water use, supply chain conditions, employee health and safety
- Improving these reduces costs, improves reliability, and enhances reputation simultaneously
- Example: reducing packaging waste lowers cost AND improves environmental reputation

### 3. Enabling Local Cluster Development
- Invest in the quality of the business environment in clusters where the company operates
- Supporting local suppliers, education, infrastructure
- Creates a stronger operating environment for the company itself
- Example: Nestlé's farmer support programs improving supply quality and farmer livelihoods

## CSV and Corporate Reputation

CSV creates reputation through a fundamentally different mechanism than traditional CSR:
- **Traditional CSR**: "We give back" — reputation built on generosity
- **CSV**: "Our business model creates value for society" — reputation built on credibility and integration

CSV is harder to fake because it requires genuine business model change, not just donation or messaging.

**Reputation implications of CSV:**
- Stronger CA associations (the business is better because of the social mission)
- Stronger CSR associations (the social impact is genuine, not bolted on)
- Harder for competitors to replicate (requires structural business model change)

---

## CSV and Employer Brand

CSV is one of the most powerful employer brand tools available:
- Candidates — especially Millennials and Gen Z — actively seek employers with a genuine social mission
- CSV provides a credible, authentic answer to "Why does this company exist beyond profit?"
- Internal EVP component: "Purpose & Impact" — CSV gives this real content
- Employees who believe in the mission show higher engagement and lower attrition

---

## Collective Impact (HBR, 2016)

The collective impact model extends CSV to multi-stakeholder collaboration:
- Complex social problems require multiple organisations working together
- No single company can solve systemic challenges alone
- Shared measurement, mutually reinforcing activities, and continuous communication are required
- Relevant for large brands operating in categories with systemic challenges (food, energy, finance, health)

---

## Limitations and Critiques of CSV

1. **Optimistic about business motivation**: Not all companies can align profit with social good
2. **Difficult to scale**: CSV requires structural change; many companies default to CSR as easier
3. **Risk of greenwashing**: The CSV label can be applied to superficial initiatives
4. **Measurement challenge**: Shared value is harder to measure than traditional financial ROI

**For brand strategy**: The test of authentic CSV is whether the social impact would continue even if it weren't good for reputation. If the answer is no, it is CSR in CSV clothing.
